Experience

Data science associate

Working at JP Morgan Chase to prevent fraud and analyze risk in Chase customer banking. I work in the credit, auto and consumer risk space.

Machine learning engineer

At BD I had the opportunity to work as a Data Scientist for the Summer of 2017. My team built a BD portfolio web application for drug price monitoring and budget management using predictive analytics in Python. We built a R tool for forecasting demand-supply variations in pharma drugs and developed optimal pricing stratergies for customers thus maximizing profit. The tool was developed to have low latency and high accuracy. It was built on a distributed platform on HDFS with Spark clusters in a Cloudera DS work-bench deployment.

Student Researcher

As a visiting researcher, I was guided by Dr. Jino Hans on the problem of Single Image Super-resolution using matrix analysis, during Summer 2016. The main idea was to retrieve image information taking into account the semantic information it carries. We extracted 2D spatial similarity kernels and improved image quality by 2.1 dB and image similarity by 22%. We published a journal paper (Article 8306342, Hindawi publications) on Image Super-Resolution using Matrix valued operators.

Summer intern - Deep learning

I was part of the prestigious Summer Research Fellowship program 2015 at Indian Institute of Technology Madras, awarded to the nation's best student researchers. I carried out a project on Advanced Driver Assistance Systems (ADAS) using Deep learning. The project aimed at implementing a full-scale Advanced Driver Assistance systems, including Pedestrian, Lane and Speed-bump detection. We implemented the Katamari deep net with SIFT ensemble features for recursive adaptation. This algorithm performed on-par with then state-of-the-art algorithms on CalTech 101 and KITTI datasets.

Winter intern - ML Lab

I was part of the DSP team at Analog Devices lab in Chennai during the Summer of 2014. My team engineered the project on auto-regressive sound equalizer using Kalman filters in AN-BF609-C processor. We achieved an 8:10 subjective equalization measure and a 22% reduction in MSE.
